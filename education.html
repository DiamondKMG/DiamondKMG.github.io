Teaching Experience

Lab courses: Vertebrate biology, human physiology, mammalogy, comparative Vertebrate morphology, SlicerMorph
Guest lectures: Vertebrate biology (Diversity of fishes: bony fishes; Dinosaurs and marine reptiles; Archosaursia); Comparative Physiology (Foraging strategies)
Content Development: Clemson Campbell Museum of Natural History (website and content for interactive display of museum exhibits); Mammalogy lab (handouts and exercises)

Mentoring Experience

Thanks to the Creative Inquiry program at Clemson and the 2016 NSF funded REU, I was able to help mentor students as they developed their own research projects. I have also been a mentor for a 2017 field study with the Erell Institute.

Students mentored

Kalyn Miles & Nathan Greenslit - Impacts of aquatic toxins on escape performance in juvenile gobies (2019)
Nikole Schneider & AJ McKamy - In stream predator attack performance using 3D videography (2017- 2020)
Grace Forker - Relationships between escape performance and vertebral morphology in goby fishes (2017-2019)

Outreach

My parents instilled a work ethic that has propelled me in my chosen career as a scienitst. However, until I went to collage, and opportunity my parents did not have, I didn't know that being a scientist was a career option. I am passionate about make sure more creatively curious kids know this option is out there and how they can do it too! If you or your kids have questions about biology, or animals, or science in general my contact info is below, please reach out! Be it in person, over Skype/Hangounts/Zoom, or evern by letter/email, I'd love to talk science with you!
